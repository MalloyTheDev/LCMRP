## 1. Research question

Can the exact registered Candidate Memory Taxonomy v0.1 satisfy its bounded term-contract, distinguish its declared lifecycle and epistemic concepts, preserve genuinely different kind-first and role-first organizational commitments, and retain governance and adversarial ambiguity across predeclared constructed cases without importing implementation assumptions or forcing favorable classifications?

## 2. Layer and boundaries

- **Layer justification:** This is a mechanism-free structural analysis of one versioned candidate vocabulary. It studies definitions, relations, classifications, and counterexamples rather than a memory implementation.
- **Subject definition:** The exact registered product-independent candidate taxonomy containing 33 versioned term IDs, ten orthogonal axes, two competing functional organizations, 20 observable distinctions, 12 competency questions, constructed edge cases, and explicit unresolved obligations.
- **Included:** term IDs and boundaries; necessary- and sufficient-condition statuses; observable distinctions; Organization K and Organization R; contextual and cross-axis classification; lifecycle, epistemic, provenance, authority, conflict, retention, forgetting, archival, and deletion semantics; ambiguity and challenge retention.
- **Excluded:** implementations; storage and retrieval performance; language or embedding models; external deployment behavior; human cognition; biological equivalence; representative population claims; human-subject and participant-data research; controlled computational or empirical evaluation; evidence synthesis; legal conclusions; production decisions.
- **Mechanism under evaluation:** None.
- **Mechanism maturity labels applicable:** None.

The case constructions are abstract research inputs. They do not prescribe a database layout, interface, service, model, provider, or application schema.

## 3. Hypothesis

- **Claim:** Under the frozen structural method, all 33 term contracts and all 20 declared distinctions can be evaluated without implementation assumptions; Organization K and Organization R produce materially different identity, cardinality, or classification commitments on at least two of four held-out organization cases across at least two comparison dimensions; and every predeclared governance obligation can retain challenge or ambiguity without a forced favorable disposition.
- **Competing explanation:** The apparent precision is editorial. Some terms lack challengeable boundaries, declared distinctions collapse under controlled cases, the two organizations differ only in wording, or governance categories force unsupported conclusions or omit material ambiguity.
- **Predicted observation:** A complete adjudicated ledger contains all required cells; all confirmatory thresholds are met; at least two held-out organization cases preserve differences across at least two dimensions; and no integrity constraint, held-out case, or adversarial case produces an unrepresentable contradiction or forced favorable inference.
- **Falsifier:** Any exact term lacks a required contract element; any declared distinction collapses or requires an undeclared assumption; fewer than two held-out organization cases or fewer than two dimensions yield materially different commitments; any case requires a vendor, product, application, or biological-equivalence assumption; any forbidden entailment is required; or any integrity failure prevents reliable interpretation.

## 5. Experimental or analytical design

- **Primary method profile:** `LCMRP-MPROF-0001-M1-STRUCTURAL-TAXONOMY@1`.
- **Units of analysis:** exact term-contract rows, declared distinction pairs, organization-by-case encodings, integrity-constraint-by-case judgments, and ambiguity-ledger entries.
- **Inputs:** the exact subject, category-evaluation rules, 20 positive cases, 12 negative cases, 16 held-out cases, configuration, and environment.
- **Evaluator structure:** two independent primary adjudicators, followed by one separate tie adjudicator only for disagreements. Authors and freeze authority are ineligible.
- **Coding values:** `SATISFIED`, `CHALLENGED`, `AMBIGUOUS`, `NOT_APPLICABLE`, and `INVALID_CASE`.
- **Controlled conditions:** exact bytes; identical evaluator packets; deterministic pseudonyms and order; no network, model inference, or stochastic sampling; fixed rubric, metrics, thresholds, and one reconciliation pass.
- **Confounders:** case construction from the candidate, evaluator familiarity with hypotheses, cueing by language, uneven category prevalence, ambiguity in natural-language definitions, and a weak baseline. Controls include source-role blinding, independent coding, locked ledgers, agreement reporting, exact citations, retention of disagreements, and bounded conclusions.
- **Held-out cases:** all 16 cases in `cases/held-out-cases.json`. They are visible but prohibited from post-freeze rule or threshold development.
- **Boundary and adversarial cases:** all 12 negative cases and held-out cases 5–16.
- **Stop or integrity-abort conditions:** digest or identity mismatch; any planned output before freeze; case or rule mutation after freeze; missing or conflicted evaluator eligibility; source-role or peer-code leakage without a clean replacement; unsupported human-subject or participant data; duplicate or missing units; or required interpretation outside this profile.

### Procedure

1. The accepted freeze action verified subject resolution, bound adjudicator eligibility and isolation criteria without inventing personnel, recorded no prior result access, computed all raw-byte digests, and published the immutable frozen manifest and attestation.
2. After freeze and before any case packet, planned output, or result access, publish and digest-lock an immutable execution intake that binds the exact frozen study record and digest; names two primary adjudicators and one tie adjudicator by stable contributor IDs; records role declarations, execution authority, and timestamp; and asserts no prior result access. Protocol, profile, rule, or case authors and the freeze authority are ineligible.
3. Before coding, recompute every frozen digest and set-compare all declared term, distinction, competency-question, integrity-constraint, source, case, baseline, and analysis IDs.
4. Generate evaluator case pseudonyms and order by the exact SHA-256 procedure in `reproducibility/configuration.json`.
5. Strip source role, path, selection rationale, stress targets, and organization-case marker from initial evaluator packets.
6. Give both primary adjudicators the same exact subject, rules, protocol, packet, and blank ledger.
7. Each primary adjudicator independently codes every required cell with exact subject and case citations and a rationale. Missing facts are never imputed.
8. Serialize and digest-lock both initial ledgers before comparison.
9. Compute analysis-level and overall raw exact agreement and Cohen's kappa. Preserve category frequencies and `NOT_ESTIMABLE` cases.
10. Reveal disagreements after lock. Permit one documented reconciliation pass. Send unresolved cells to the tie adjudicator.
11. Preserve original and final codes. Apply the confirmatory decision rules without discretionary threshold changes.
12. Produce one output ledger per analysis at its predeclared locator. If an analysis cannot run, publish one atomic terminal disposition rather than a fabricated result.
13. Publish at most one active atomic finding or disposition per analysis, cite the execution intake and contributor IDs in finding provenance, and close out only after exact set equality across all five IDs.

## 8. Metrics and analysis rules

Agreement gates apply to each analysis: raw exact agreement must be at least `0.80` and estimable Cohen's kappa must be at least `0.60`. A failed gate makes the candidate assessment `INCONCLUSIVE` even after a complete tie-adjudicated ledger. Kappa with expected agreement equal to one is reported `NOT_ESTIMABLE`, not silently passed or failed.

## 10. Security and privacy considerations

- **Assets:** exact subject and protocol identity; case-source integrity; evaluator independence; immutable execution intake; locked initial judgments; disagreement and negative-result retention; pseudonym mapping; final analysis ledgers; contributor provenance.
- **Threats:** subject or case substitution; post-freeze mutation; forged provenance; result access before freeze; selective case exclusion; case-role cueing; peer-code leakage; evaluator manipulation through rationale text; suppression of disagreements or nulls; overbroad deletion of negative material; accidental real sensitive data; over-interpretation as security assurance.
- **Controls:** raw-byte SHA-256 binding; immutable freeze attestation and post-freeze execution intake; deterministic set and count checks; source-role blinding; independent locked ledgers; separate tie adjudication; exact citations; offline execution; append-oriented retention; mandatory terminal dispositions; no result-to-claim automation; explicit claim boundaries.
- **Retention and deletion:** Retain every frozen input, initial and final ledger, digest, pseudonym map, reconciliation, tie decision, deviation, negative/null/ambiguous/invalid/not-run output, atomic record, and closeout. Corrections supersede rather than overwrite. If real sensitive data is discovered, halt, quarantine access, preserve only the minimum incident metadata permitted by governance, and obtain explicit review before any deletion or redaction; do not silently rewrite a frozen artifact.
- **Residual risks:** SHA-256 does not prove authorship or semantic correctness; repository-visible cases are not truly concealed; adjudicators know the candidate and hypotheses; small synthetic sets permit construction bias; contributor IDs reveal professional participation; a structural pass may be misrepresented as privacy or security effectiveness.
- **Human subjects involved:** No.

## 13. Limitations
