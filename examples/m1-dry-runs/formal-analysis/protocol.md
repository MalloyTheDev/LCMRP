## Hypothesis

The exact synthetic theory `{A}` will have a retained satisfying valuation; it will entail proposition `A`; and it will not entail proposition `B`, for which the complete four-valuation search will retain a countermodel.

## Related mechanisms or prior work

No mechanism is under evaluation. The only governing prior artifact is the accepted Foundational Study Contract v0.1 and its `FORMAL_ANALYSIS` profile obligations.

## Experimental design

Freeze a two-atom classical propositional system, enumerate all four valuations, test theory satisfaction, test the intended entailment, and retain a countermodel for the declared non-entailment. The analysis is deterministic and uses no participant data, dataset, language model, network service, theorem prover, or external solver.

## Baseline

The rejection baseline is an incomplete or merely syntactic check. The semantic dry run succeeds only when every frozen valuation is represented exactly once and the declared semantic decision rules can be replayed from retained outputs.

## Scenarios

The complete scenario domain is `{A=false,B=false}`, `{A=false,B=true}`, `{A=true,B=false}`, and `{A=true,B=true}`.

## Metrics and decision rules

- Satisfiability: number of retained theory models must be at least one.
- Intended entailment: zero theory models may falsify `PROP-9902-A`.
- Declared non-entailment: at least one retained theory model must falsify `PROP-9902-B`.
- Coverage: exactly four distinct valuations must be retained.

Missing or duplicate valuations invalidate the affected analysis. A missing satisfying valuation rejects the satisfiability claim. A theory model falsifying `A` rejects the intended-entailment claim. Absence of a retained `B=false` theory model rejects the declared non-entailment claim.
